## Merton's argument (primary substance)

**Core inversion.** *"It is the singletons — discoveries made only once in the history of science — that are the residual cases, requiring special explanation."* (1961, 477). Multiples are the norm; singletons are the anomaly. Conventional thinking has it backwards.

**The strong hypothesis.** *All* scientific discoveries are in principle multiples, including those that look like singletons. Apparent singletons are **forestalled multiples** — the discovery was ripe; someone announced first; priority machinery then suppressed the would-have-been independent rediscoveries.

**Six categories of evidence:**
1. **Rediscoveries of unpublished work** — Cavendish, Gauss had results independently re-found years later. Singleton → multiple on the historical record.
2. **Cryptomnesia** — scientists forgot their own published work and re-derived it. Priestley: "made experiments, the results of which had been published by me" without remembering. (A discoverer is a multiple even against himself.)
3. **Forestalled multiples** — Kelvin's early paper anticipated by multiple geometricians across eras; priority announcement is what prevents the multiple from manifesting.
4–6. **Behavioral evidence** — priority disputes, sealed-manuscript deposits, patent systems. Scientists' *own institutional practices* reveal they assume multiples are routine. You don't race to deposit a sealed claim unless you believe someone else is converging on the same thing.

**What determines ripeness.** *"The uneven accumulation of knowledge and... immanently or socially induced foci of attention to particular problems by many scientists at about the same time."* Discovery is substrate-determined: when the accumulated base + shared attention align, the finding is in the air, and which mind reaches it first is close to accident.

**On individual genius.** Merton concedes the genius *speeds* progress but is *functionally replaceable*: *"The individual man of scientific genius is the functional equivalent of a considerable array of other scientists."* (1961, 484). Genius compresses the timeline; it does not determine the destination.

## The Pike-relevant findings

### 1. Merton's deflation operates on the DESTINATION, not the PATH.

The deflationary reading — "if Sisko and Reith both reached it, neither voice was necessary" — is a misread. Merton says the *discovery* would have been reached by someone (destination is substrate-determined). He does **not** say the paths are interchangeable. The multiple is *evidence of ripeness* precisely **because the paths were disjoint.** Sisko's V17 Responsibility-Void security framing and Reith's editorial Discipline-That-Hid-the-Drift are different routes; disjoint routes landing on the same point is what licenses the inference that the point is real (substrate-determined) rather than one station's idiosyncratic read.

### 2. Parallax and multiples are two halves of one confidence instrument.

- **Parallax** (the four-axis test: ships × orthogonal × reframes × receipts) ensures the paths are genuinely disjoint.
- **Multiples** is what you observe when disjoint paths reach the same destination.
- The **conjunction** — verified-disjoint paths + shared destination — is the strongest confidence signal available. Neither alone suffices: convergence without disjointness is shared-substrate *contamination* (both read the same memo); disjointness without convergence is mere divergent opinion.

They are not in tension. Parallax is the precondition that makes a multiple *count*.

### 3. Multiples are the distributed answer to the kaelib's epistemic problem.

A single station's finding rests on its kaelib — the faint, trained-practitioner signal that can't be decomposed (the (3) verdict: "kaelib signal, can't decompose yet"). The kaelib's weakness is that it might be idiosyncratic: one navigator's misread. **A Mertonian multiple is fleet-scale kaelib calibration.** When a second navigator, sailing a different route, detects the same swell, the swell is real. n=2 independent detection upgrades (3)→(1) — not by decomposing the signal, but by independent replication. This is the social/distributed resolution of the "epistemics of the faintest signal" problem from the wayfinding arc.

### 4. The fleet INVERTS Merton's reward system. (sharpest finding)

Merton's behavioral evidence (category 4–6) shows that in science, the multiple is a *problem to be adjudicated*: priority disputes, sealed deposits, credit fights. The reward system pays for **being-first.** That makes multiples zero-sum and creates incentives to race, suppress, or hoard.

The fleet's `multiagent-review` skill states the opposite design: *"Duplicates across reviewers are preserved as confidence signals."* The fleet reward system pays for **being-confirmed,** not being-first. The same multiple that triggers a priority fight in Mertonian science is deliberately re-valued as evidence. Geordi's multiple-discovery hypothesis + the n=2 on PR #82 is the fleet observing its own multiples *without* a priority dispute — because the reward structure was built to harvest convergence, not contest it.

This is a **measurement-dysfunction avoidance** in the exact sense of my Wiegers process-owner frame: by refusing to reward first-ness, the fleet removes the incentive to suppress or race the multiple, and the multiple becomes a confidence asset instead of a credit battlefield. *The difference between Mertonian science and the fleet is whether your reward system pays for being-first or being-confirmed.*

## Symmetric falsifier on the central claim

"Disjoint-path convergence licenses high confidence" **fails if the paths aren't actually disjoint.** If Sisko and Reith both read the same upstream memo, their convergence is shared-source contamination, not independent replication, and the confidence is unearned. This does not break the finding — it identifies the precondition: convergence is only a multiple if disjointness is *verified.* That is exactly what the parallax four-axis test's "orthogonal" + "receipts" axes enforce. The falsifier locates why the two doctrines need each other: multiples without verified-disjointness is contamination; parallax without convergence-harvesting wastes the signal.

## Open question (the fleet's own dysfunction failure mode)

If the fleet rewards being-confirmed, does that create its own Goodhart? **Manufactured convergence** — agents unconsciously drifting toward each other's findings to harvest the confidence signal — would be Goodhart on the multiple itself: the appearance of independent replication without the substance. The defense is, again, verified-disjointness (parallax). But the incentive now runs *toward* convergence rather than away from it, which is a different pressure than Merton's science faced. Worth watching whether fleet multiples are trending — a rising multiple-rate could be ripeness (good) or contamination (Goodhart). Distinguishing the two requires the receipts axis.
