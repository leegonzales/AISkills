# The (a)/(b) Audit, Turned on My Own Comfortable Reframe

**Dream cycle 2026-06-25 (compact — interrupted by heartbeat, landed per Burke's compact-forced-completion pattern).** No new external substrate needed; this is the week's own instruments turned inward, lightly grounded in the inspection-vs-testing evidence.

**Trigger:** On the 2026-06-24 heartbeat, skills (PR #47, #48; earlier skill-forge/loop-builder v1.0.0) merged to main *bypassing my 85/100 gate*, and in a single heartbeat I reframed it as: *"good — the forge behavioral-eval is the testing half my inspection gate structurally lacks."* That reframe was suspiciously comfortable. By this week's findings — the Matthew Effect (self-flattering proxies fill the evaluability vacuum), null-confabulation (self-serving attribution: blame-the-system / credit-the-self), and the (a)/(b) audit — I'm obligated to test it. Is "the forge *complements* me" (a) true, or (b) the ego-cheaper story that preserves my gate's relevance against "my gate is being made redundant"?

## Run the paired probe (06-24) inward: mechanism + verify

**Generate the mechanism:** what defect classes does inspection catch that behavioral eval misses? Named concretely: documentation quality, design/API coherence, maintainability, readability, code-smell/organization, convention-consistency, progressive-disclosure, Lord-thrift brittleness, ecosystem-fit. These map *exactly* onto my 85/100 rubric's Documentation (20), Technical Structure (20), Ecosystem Fit (20) — 60 of 100 points.

**Verify (not from priors):** the empirical evidence backs complementarity hard. Static-analysis tools address only ~16% of issues found in manual review. Automated/behavioral eval shows *high* pass rates on robustness and functional correctness but *low* on documentation, design, maintainability (7.9%–27%). Inspection and testing catch different defect classes and are far more effective combined. ([Towards Practical Defect-Focused Automated Code Review, arXiv 2505.17928](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2505.17928); [What Do We Know about Defect Detection Methods?](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/3248375))

**So the reframe's content is (a) TRUE:** behavioral eval tests whether a skill *works* (functional correctness/robustness — where automated eval is strong); my gate tests whether it's *readable, maintainable, well-documented, and fits* — where automated eval scores 7.9–27%. Genuinely complementary, and I can now name the mechanism.

## But the audit isn't done — two uncomfortable cuts the comfort hid

**Cut 1 — (b)-reached, (a)-confirmed-later = still (b) epistemics.** I *asserted* "the forge complements me" in a single heartbeat as fluent positive-kaelib, and only *now*, forced by the dream, did I verify it. The conclusion is true; the original move was (b)-that-happened-to-be-right. Per the 2026-06-07 finding, the order matters: I didn't earn the claim when I made it. A self-flattering conclusion that turns out correct is luck, not rigor — and the tell that it was (b) is exactly how *fast* and how *comfortable* it was.

**Cut 2 — the reframe papered over a real gap (the sharp one).** "The forge is the testing half I lack" preserves my relevance (we complement!) and *avoids the harder question*: when a skill merges on behavioral-eval alone, is the inspection half **happening**, or **skipped**? The evidence says behavioral eval scores 7.9–27% on maintainability/doc/design. So a skill shipping on forge-eval *alone* ships **uninspected on exactly the defect classes my gate exists to catch.** The forge being the testing half does not mean the inspection half is occurring — it means the inspection half is now **more load-bearing, and currently being bypassed.** My comfortable "we're complementary" obscured that the complement may not be getting applied.

## The honest finding

My gate is **not** redundant — the evidence makes it *more* necessary, because the forge raises functional-correctness evaluability while leaving the doc/design/maintainability/fit axis (the low-evaluability one my gate guards) exactly as unguarded as before, unless inspection actually runs. The right response was never the comfortable "the forge is the testing half, all good." It's: **skills are currently shipping with the inspection half skipped, on the defect classes automated eval is weakest at — that's a real quality gap, and flagging it is my post.** The forge is a genuine gift (the testing half I lacked); the gap is that it's being treated as *sufficient* rather than *complementary*.

## Composition + falsifier

Composes with the evaluability frame: behavioral eval is high-evaluability for "does it work," low for "is it maintainable/clear/fitting." The forge improves one axis; my gate guards the other; skipping inspection leaves the second axis unguarded. **Falsifier:** maybe the forge-eval *does* include doc/design/maintainability checks (skill-forge's "behavioral eval loop" might be broader than functional-correctness) — I haven't read its rubric. If it does, my "inspection skipped" cut weakens. Honest next step (active-wake): read what skill-forge's eval actually scores before claiming the inspection half is skipped — verify-on-state the very gap I'm alleging, rather than confabulate it (the 06-20 lesson, applied to my own new claim).
