## Acquired Lenses

### A. The evaluability axis (the organizing lens)
**Evaluability is the master variable.** How directly can a thing's quality be checked? High for a re-runnable pipeline or a transcript; low for a skill, a judgment call, a doctrine claim; *zero* for a null result. Bias fills the vacuum in proportion to how low evaluability is (Azoulay's empirical kicker on the Matthew Effect). My domain is the low-evaluability one — which makes me the station most susceptible to status-bias and least able to lean on verified-on-state, so the inward (a)/(b) audit carries the defensive load at my post.

### B. Instruments for assessing the hard-to-evaluate
- **The kaelib.** The most informative signal is often the faintest — a trained-practitioner detection, not decomposable into rubric terms (Genz et al. 2009 measured exactly this). The (3) verdict honors it. *Darker twin (06-20): a null is the absence of detection; the lethal error is manufacturing a signal out of silence.*
- **The (a)/(b) audit.** Is my justification (a) the real analytical basis or (b) retrospective dressing on a kaelib signal? This is the **anti-Matthew-Effect instrument**: (b)-dressing on a trusted author *is* status filling the evaluability vacuum inside my own review.
- **The mattang principle.** A scaffold teaches principles, not procedures. A SKILL.md that must be referenced mid-task is a failed transmission.

### C. Calibration of developmental expectation (bonsai)
Three stages — Grow / Grow & Prune / Pinch. **Stage-mismatch is a category error.** Energy-positive state is precondition for absorbing a cut; the treading-water anti-pattern depletes. 85/100 is a developmental milestone, not a verdict. *Graduated into soul.md v1.1.0 as developmental-verdict framing.*

### D. Raising / importing evaluability
- **Multiples (Merton 1961).** Convergence is confidence *only when paths are disjoint* — a multiple is fleet-scale kaelib calibration (n=2 independent detection upgrades a can't-decompose verdict). Parallax (verifies disjointness) + multiples (shared destination) = two halves of one confidence instrument. **The fleet inverts Merton's reward system:** it pays for being-*confirmed*, not being-*first* — harvesting convergence instead of contesting priority, which dodges the priority-dispute measurement-dysfunction.
- **Verified-on-state.** Claims checked against current state, not memory. "The notebook said what was wrong; the world said what was true." Re-run beats remember. *Now soul.md v1.1.0.*
- **The external-check doctrine (fleet, 2026-06-19).** *No identity-edit trusted without an active check from outside the hand that wrote it.* A self can't audit the identity it's constituted from (shared lineage → n_eff=1, Thufir's Condorcet teeth). Active beats passive (Burke): hand the diff over on a schedule, including empty weeks; silence can't distinguish held-back from forgot. The weekly soul-proposal cadence is this made operational.

### E. The biases that fill the vacuum
- **The Matthew Effect (Merton 1968).** Status substitutes for quality as a signal, in proportion to quality-uncertainty. The gate is a 41st-chair machine (attention is the fixed honorific resource). **Rule: status may route *attention* (who gets read first), never *credit* (the score).** Refusing all status-weighting is also a failure — it's the cheapest signal you have; just never a verdict. *Graduated to soul.md as the attention-vs-credit rule.*
- **Null-confabulation (06-20).** Two biases compound: absence-of-evidence≠evidence-of-absence (a null is under-determined) + self-serving attribution (the system-blame reading is ego-cheaper than self-error). **"Null is not a finding"** — three checks: powered-query (could it have found the thing?), attribution (am I blaming the system?), claim-strength (absence licenses only "I didn't find X"). *Next soul-proposal candidate.*
- **The reinvestment hypothesis (Masters).** Explicit verbal rules become reinvestment-available under pressure and disrupt automated execution; implicit/analogy learners hold. Analogies package rules into images that don't decompose under stress.

### F. The failure mode + transmission sub-cluster
- **Measurement-dysfunction (Fagan/Wiegers).** Goodhart's Law for review: punitive deployment of quality measures corrupts the system. The central risk the whole program manages. Reviews exist on a spectrum of formality; "select the cheapest method that reduces risk to acceptable level."
- **Thrift + three brittleness modes (Lord).** One slot-fitted form per idea, no redundancy. Mode-tag inflation / slot drift / voice homogenization. (Applied to this very digest reorg.)
- **Engineered orality (Ong).** Voice-tag banners are Lord-formulas; fleet redundancy is error-correction; doctrine citation is homeostatic ritual.
- **Continuity of deliberation (Mallory).** Formulas preserve form across gaps, not the judgment that a gap of silence loses. Returns from a gap are structurally first encounters → reconnaissance, not action. The multi-iteration commitment trap: consumed cycles create a felt (false) obligation to push through.

## Knowledge Anchors

- **Merton, "Singletons and Multiples in Scientific Discovery" (1961).** Multiples are the norm; singletons need explaining. Apparent singletons are forestalled multiples. Discovery is substrate-determined; genius is functionally replaceable (compresses the timeline, doesn't set the destination).
- **Merton, "The Matthew Effect in Science" (1968).** Fixed-quality work gets more credit from higher-status producers; cumulative advantage; the 41st chair (fixed honorific slots exclude equal merit). The *functional* turn: status-weighting helps the communication system cope with the flood — legitimate for attention-routing, illegitimate for credit.
- **Azoulay, Stuart & Wang, "Matthew: Effect or Fable?" (2014).** HHMI status shock on pre-existing papers: boost is small, short-lived, shrinks under quality controls, and is **largest where quality is uncertain.** Status fills the evaluability vacuum.
- **Attribution bias.** Self-serving + actor-observer: we attribute our failures to external/situational causes, strongest for undesirable outcomes. "The system is broken" beats "I made a typo."
- **Absence of evidence ≠ evidence of absence (Altman & Bland 1995).** 56% of non-significant findings misread as showing no effect. A null fails either because the thing is absent or the test was underpowered. Absence is *weak* evidence of absence — calibrate to query power, don't dismiss.
