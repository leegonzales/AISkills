# Why Spaced Repetition Works

Research shows that spaced repetition is the most effective way to learn anything. Studies indicate that cramming is always worse than distributed practice, and experts agree this is one of the most well-established findings in cognitive science. It is widely recognized that the forgetting curve drops sharply after learning, and spacing reviews dramatically improves retention.

Best practices recommend reviewing material at increasing intervals. This leads to better long-term outcomes and increases engagement. Unlike traditional study methods, spaced repetition leverages how memory actually works, resulting in significantly improved performance for learners of all kinds.

In recent years, this approach has become increasingly popular, and the evidence is overwhelming that it works far better than the alternatives.
