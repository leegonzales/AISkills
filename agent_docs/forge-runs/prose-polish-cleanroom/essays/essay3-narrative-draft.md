# Draft 3 — Personal narrative (genre: reflective). Meandering, telling-not-showing draft, to be polished. [HOLDOUT]

I have always loved the ocean ever since I was a kid. There is just something about it that makes me feel calm and happy and also kind of small in a good way. My grandfather was a fisherman and he used to take me out on his boat when I was young, and those are some of my favorite memories from my childhood, even though it was a long time ago now.

He taught me a lot of things about the sea and about life in general. He was a very wise man and he didn't talk that much, but when he did say something it was usually important and worth listening to. I remember he once told me that the ocean doesn't care about your plans, and I think about that a lot, especially now that I am older and have experienced more of the ups and downs that life tends to throw at you.

These days I don't get to go to the ocean as much as I would like to because life gets busy and I live pretty far away from the coast now. But whenever I do get the chance to go back and stand by the water, all of those old feelings come back and I remember my grandfather and everything he taught me. It makes me feel grateful and a little sad at the same time, which is a complicated feeling but also a meaningful one.
